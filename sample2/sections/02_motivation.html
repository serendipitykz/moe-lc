Why MoE + Long Context?

Mixture of Experts (MoE)

Activates only \(k\) of \(N_{\text{exp}}\) experts per token, slashing active FLOPs while keeping total model capacity high.

Example: Mixtral-8×7B activates 2 of 8 experts per token — same inference cost as a ~13B dense model, but 47B total parameters of knowledge.

\[ \text{Active FLOPs} \approx \frac{k}{N_{\text{exp}}} \times \text{Dense FLOPs} \]

Long Context

Extends sequence length \(L\) to 128K – 1M tokens, enabling document-level and repository-scale reasoning in a single forward pass.

Key challenge: standard attention is \(\mathcal{O}(L^2)\) in memory. Techniques like FlashAttention, RoPE extrapolation, and chunk-wise recurrence tame this cost.

\[ L \in [128\text{K},\; 1\text{M}] \text{ tokens} \]

Together: capable models that stay compute-efficient at scale.
